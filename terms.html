Terms

Ethics

One example of an ethical concept that connects to our project is Utilitarianism. Utilitarianism means that the action that causes the greatest amount of good for the greatest amount of people is the ethical course of action to take. Not only does combating ocean pollution benefit people, but also it benefits the marine life currently being affected by said pollution.

Economics

  • Consumer Sovereignty
  • Trade-off
  • Demand
Currently, there is a significant demand for plastic products and other products that pollute our oceans and beaches. By finding out which companies are responsible for pollution, we, as a society, can use our consumer sovereignty to boycott these products and support businesses that do not pollute. If we use our consumer sovereignty to diminish the demand for plastic and products of other polluting companies, there will obviously be a trade-off in convenience and possibly price.

Aims

  • Engaged Citizenship

Throughout this senior capstone project, our awareness of engaged citizenship has been greatly strengthened. Throughout our time in Hawaii, and Punahou, we have heard many advertisements and speeches about plastic in the ocean and environmental issues related with it. However, we rarely hear or see people from our age group taking action. While there are a select few who are passionate about plastic pollution, the overwhelming majority of students sit back and think “someone else will do it.” If everybody were to adopt this mentality, our future generations will be drastically impacted. Therefore, we feel the idea of engaged citizenship represents how we should be taking action to become engaged in the effort to maintain and protect our home.

As we became actively engaged in the movement, we quickly realized how grand the problem truly is. Although we hauled in hundreds of pounds of plastic and other pollution from the beach during fieldwork, these efforts have only begun to scratch the surface. The beautiful beaches that we helped to clean were once again covered in pollution a mere week later. This experience helped to put the extremity of the issue into perspective. Only by getting engaged and making a real effort to effect change will we see a difference. 
​
  • Global Perspective

​Additionally, this project helped to strengthen the aim of a global perspective. While picking up over 400 pounds of pollution during field work, we quickly realized that plastic pollution is not only a local issue, but a worldwide issue. The large amounts of pollution washing up on our shores is a result of materials being washed into the ocean and getting pushed to our islands. Therefore, it is not only our responsibility in Hawaii to fight the pollution, but the world as a whole to eliminate the issue altogether.

Over the course of our lives, we plan to use our global perspectives to influence others to take action in the fight against ocean and beach pollution.
